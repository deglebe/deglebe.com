ultimately, the job of a programmer is not to focus strictly on output of a certain quantity/quality of code, but rather to solve problems purely out of curiosity. this is a form of play. otherwise we waste away, lose interest, and resent our path

projects

  • sudoku - sudoku engine/puzzle generator in c99+raylib. generates varying difficulties of puzzles using a custom implemenation of the dancing links algorithm and fast backtracking solving at *quite* fast speeds.

  • got - tui git staging manager/commit authoring tool in golang+bubbletea. makes managing branches, commit messages, and tracking files somewhat easier without too much fuzz.

  • task-dag - a directed acyclic graph-based todo list manager in c-like c++, built with the unix philosophy in mind. outputs pretty graph-viz plots of tasks.

  • Sea - learning compiler development by writing one entirely from scratch in go.

  • this site - a major test of my awk knowledge

  • tmscripts - making the web a little more usable for me

  • graphdb - a simple json-based graph database which i wrote for work, basically just a set of json querying algorithms
